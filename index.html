◆Sound travels through steel approximately 15 times faster than through air — 5,120 m/s versus 343 m/s. Early railway workers would press an ear to the track to detect approaching trains long before the noise reached them through the air.
◆The smell of rain — petrichor — can be detected by the human nose at concentrations as low as 0.4 parts per trillion. The scent comes from geosmin, a compound released by soil bacteria when raindrops hit dry earth and kick microscopic particles into the air.
