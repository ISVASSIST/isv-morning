Researchers at MIT have discovered that under specific conditions, a chaotic scatter of laser light will spontaneously self-organise into a tightly focused 'pencil beam' — a paradoxical phenomenon that shouldn't work according to conventional optics. ◆Sea otters hold hands while they sleep — a behaviour known as 'rafting.' Without an anchor, they'd drift apart from their mate in open water. Pairs clasp paws to stay together, and some otters also wrap themselves in kelp fronds to keep from floating away overnight.
◆Honey never spoils. Archaeologists have found 3,000-year-old honey sealed in Egyptian tomb vessels that was still perfectly edible. Its extreme acidity, very low moisture content, and naturally occurring hydrogen peroxide create an environment where bacteria simply cannot survive.
◆The word 'salary' comes from the Latin 'salarium' — the allowance given to Roman soldiers specifically to buy salt. Salt was so valuable in the ancient world it functioned as currency across multiple civilisations. 'Worth his salt' entered English directly from this tradition.
